hi ≥70th pct, lo ≤30th, strictly-prior profiles. T-60 prices. BE 52.4%.

## Conclusions (2026-07-16)

1. **The guard/press narrative is OVER-priced.** guard_out × pressing (TO-forcing)
   opponent = only 51-53% backing the attacker; guard_out × NON-pressing opponent
   = 54-55% / +3.6-5.3%. The public story ("backup PG vs press = blowout") moves
   the line too far; the quiet version keeps the edge.
2. **big_out tier refined:** attacker paint-share is the driver (62.3% premium
   tier, Brief #1); attacker OREB-heaviness is NOT (55.1% vs 59.1% for not-hi).
3. **Fragile handle combo:** guard_out from an already TO-prone team → back
   attacker 55.4% / +5.9% (n=287, 3/4 seasons).
4. **3-heavy dogs are BAD ATS**, not good — home 3-heavy dogs +4 or more cover
   only 46.5% (n=846). The variance narrative is over-bet; low-3 dogs cover
   more (51.8%). Candidate fade lens, TRACK.
5. Standalone clashes (TO-vs-TO, OREB-vs-weak-DREB) and pace battles are
   priced. "Both slow" games lean OVER (52.1%) — market over-shades slow
   matchups — but sub-breakeven.
6. 5-channel composite is WEAKER than the 3-channel scoring composite (+2.4%
   vs +3.6%) — rebounding/TO channels dilute; keep 3P/paint/FT as canonical.
