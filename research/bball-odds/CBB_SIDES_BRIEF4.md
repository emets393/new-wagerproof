# CBB Sides Brief #4 — narrative fades, spots, rematches, coverage

46,060 team-games. T-60 prices. BE 52.4%.

## Conclusions (2026-07-16)

**CBB situational/narrative handicapping is PRICED.** Letdown, lookahead,
revenge, blowout momentum, ATS-repeat rematch angles, long road trips,
November MTEs: all ~50-52% with era-flips, nothing durable. The lone whisper
(lookahead + big favorite → fade, +0.7%) is not bettable. Coverage subsets:
the odds feed barely contains low-book games (n≤65), so "soft coverage"
isn't testable here — and KP-edge stays dead even split by coverage.

**Addendum — availability dynamics (run same day):**
- BIG RETURNED → both directions priced (49.7%/50.3%). No return edge.
- STALE absence (2nd+ game without the big) → fade only 51.4%. **The edge is
  FRESH-ONLY: the market catches up after exactly one game.** This nails the
  mechanism — it's a NEWS LAG, not a mispricing of shorthanded teams.
- Conference tier: fresh big_out fade = 58.1%/+10.9% mid/low conf (n=661) vs
  55.6%/+6.1% power conf (n=90) — softer info markets, bigger edge, but
  positive everywhere.

**The CBB sides thesis, settled: the market's failure is PERSONNEL NEWS
PROPAGATION (one-game lag, worse in smaller conferences), not situational
or relational handicapping. All future CBB sides work should orbit roster
events: absences (validated), returns of SECONDARY players (untested),
foul-out carryover, suspensions, transfers mid-season.**
