## Conclusions (2026-07-16)

**The confluence stack now mirrors MAMMOTH — rare, high-conviction tiers on a
validated base (small n = conditional slices of the n=751/724 base signals,
every tier positive all 4 seasons):**

CBB big_out fade ATS ladder:
- base 57.8%/+10.4% (n=751)
- × paint-heavy attacker: 62.3%/+19.1% (n=247)
- × thin bench (≤30th pct KenPom bench minutes): 61.8%/+17.8% (n=136)
- × tall center lost (own Hgt5 ≥70th): 59.2%/+13.0% (n=223)
- **× paint attacker AND thin bench: 75.0%/+43.2% (n=88; 73/85/65/77 by
  season)** — the MAMMOTH-grade cell: no big, no depth, opponent attacks paint
- CONFERENCE games (Jan+) 59.5% vs nonconf 54.2% — market softest on
  mid-season roster news; rest is not a factor (both splits ≈ base)

NBA big_out game-OVER ladder:
- STAR big (top-minutes AND top-rebounder) out: 57.7%/+10.1% (n=307, all 4)
- role big out: 52.8% — the tier IS the star
- **STAR big × FT-drawing attacker: 67.1%/+28.1% (n=79, all 4 positive)**
- multi regulars out × pace: 56.1%/+7.0%

**Sleeper find — the TRANSFER-PORTAL signal:** fading low-continuity away
rosters in Nov-Dec loses -14.7% (n=830) → i.e. BACKING portal-rebuilt away
teams early season wins ≈55.3%, consistent and growing in 24-25/25-26. The
market is slow to rate rebuilt rosters. Promote to its own study.

Priced/dead: raw height clashes, experience edges, tall+paint standalone
(~50-53%). Roster attributes only matter as TIERS on live signals.
